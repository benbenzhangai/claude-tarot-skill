# Example Tarot Reading

This is an example of what a reading from the Tarot Reflection skill looks like.

---

## Example Request

**User:** "Do a 3-card reading about whether I should accept a job offer at a startup. I'm currently at a stable corporate job."

---

## Claude's Response

### Tarot Reading: Career Transition Decision (Startup vs. Corporate Stability)

**Spread**: 3-card (Past/Present/Future)  
**Reversals**: Enabled

---

#### Cards Drawn

1. **Past**: Eight of Pentacles (Upright)
2. **Present**: Two of Swords (Upright)
3. **Future**: The Chariot (Upright)

---

#### Interpretations

**Past — Eight of Pentacles (Upright)**: You've been investing significant energy in skill-building and steady, focused work. This foundation of competence and dedication has brought you to this decision point. The craftsmanship you've developed in your current role is not in question—it's a solid base that's enabled this opportunity to even exist.

**Present — Two of Swords (Upright)**: You're in a genuine stalemate, deliberately avoiding the decision while weighing competing priorities. There's a sense of conflicting information or values (stability vs. growth, security vs. excitement) that has you in analysis paralysis. The decision feels high-stakes, and you're trying to see clearly through the uncertainty, but indecision itself is becoming the primary obstacle.

**Future — The Chariot (Upright)**: If you commit to a direction—either direction—you have the discipline and willpower to succeed. This card isn't telling you which path to choose; it's saying that victory comes through decisive commitment and harnessing your competing desires (security AND growth) rather than being torn between them. The momentum and clarity come after the choice, not before it.

---

#### Synthesis

You've built real competence through dedicated work (Eight of Pentacles), but that very foundation has created a choice point that feels paralyzing (Two of Swords). The dominant theme here is Swords energy (mental, decision-making) coupled with Pentacles (material, career). The tension is clear: your past work ethic isn't the question—your ability to commit to a direction is. The Chariot suggests that whichever path you choose, success depends on decisive action and integrating your values (not abandoning one for the other). Indecision is the bottleneck, not lack of capability.

---

#### Actionable Guidance

- **Set a decision deadline**: Give yourself a specific date (e.g., 2 weeks from today) and identify the 3 most critical pieces of information you need before then.
- **Run a "commitment test"**: Schedule a detailed conversation with the startup's hiring manager to gauge your actual excitement level when discussing the role in depth.
- **Quantify the trade-offs**: Create a simple spreadsheet comparing salary, equity, learning opportunities, risk tolerance, and lifestyle impact across both options.
- **Test your fear**: Journal on "What am I most afraid will happen if I choose the startup?" and "What am I most afraid will happen if I stay?" to identify if fear is driving the stalemate.
- **Consult your "board of advisors"**: Talk to 2-3 people whose judgment you trust who have made similar transitions, and explicitly ask them what factors they weighed most heavily.
- **Define your "win condition"**: What would make either choice feel successful in 12 months? Write this down for both paths.

---

#### Guardrails & Reality Check

This reading is reflective guidance, not fate or certainty. Use it to sharpen your thinking, but validate insights through real-world experiments and concrete information. You have agency; the cards illuminate dynamics (indecision as the current block, capability to succeed either way), they don't dictate outcomes. 

The Chariot doesn't guarantee success at the startup—it suggests you have the discipline to make either path work if you commit. Gather the financial data (runway, equity terms, salary delta), assess the team and product-market fit, and make sure you're not romanticizing either "new adventure" or "stable comfort." This is a significant career decision—consult with mentors, financial advisors, and people who know both industries well.

**Bottom line**: The cards suggest the problem isn't your capability or even which choice is "right"—it's that not choosing is costing you momentum. Set a deadline, gather specific data, and commit.

## What Makes This a Good Reading

### ✅ Position-Aware Interpretation
- Past card interpreted as foundation/background
- Present card interpreted as current dynamic/challenge
- Future card interpreted as potential trajectory, not certainty

### ✅ Synthesized Narrative
- Identified dominant theme (decision paralysis from position of strength)
- Connected cards into coherent story
- Avoided just listing card meanings

### ✅ Actionable Guidance
- 6 specific, testable next steps
- Time-bounded ("2 weeks from today")
- Designed to reduce uncertainty (gather data, test assumptions)
- Framed with agency (what user can do)

### ✅ Appropriate Guardrails
- Frames as reflection, not prediction
- Encourages validation with real-world data
- Acknowledges uncertainty
- Suggests consulting professionals (mentors, financial advisors)
- Reaffirms user agency

### ✅ No Fortune-Telling Language
- ❌ "You will succeed at the startup"
- ✅ "You have the discipline to make either path work if you commit"

### ✅ Context-Specific
- Directly addresses the user's specific question (startup vs corporate)
- Incorporates relevant factors (stability, risk, growth)
- Provides domain-relevant advice (equity terms, product-market fit)
